Everyday Design: Reading and Response

1. Affordances & Signifiers: What are the real affordances of your micro-project? How are those affordances signified? If someone encountered your system cold, how would they know what they can do? Where are affordances hidden or unclear?

The main affordance of my micro-project is the manipulation of visuals (specifically typography). This affordance is indicated by the text box and display which signal to the user that these are the main objects of focus. With this being said, there are no explicit instructions or explanation for the tool itself, making the affordance of manipulation and distortion relatively unclear.

2. Feedback: Where does your system provide feedback? Is it immediate? Legible? Sufficient? Identify at least one moment where your system fails to communicate state clearly. What would Norman criticize?

There is very little feedback in my designed system, since the overall premise of the site is that it is a tool to be used and therefore molded by the user. Feedback would impose a certain direction on the user which would be averse to my goal of the interaction. One area where I feel there is a failure to communicate is when there are misclicks or areas which are far away enough from the text to make any impact. I think Norman would criticize this in the sense that it fails to guide the user towards understanding why their actions are resulting in the current response (or lack thereof).

3. Mapping: Is the relationship between input and outcome intuitive? Where might a user hesitate or misinterpret what is happening? Is any confusion intentional? If so, how is it structured?

I think it is. When I had initially developed my drafts of the Amoeba site/micro-project, I had plans of creating controls which would be able to push or pull from the mouse-position. This was eventually simplified to just pushing away. I find this to be more intuitive as it is a similar reaction to that of pressing down in something as the body of objects or substance around what you press down will likely spread out to circle the area which you now take up. I think that users may hesitate with their usage if they are unsure about how much distortion that they would like. It's from this angle that I want to create inputs for people to control the distortion levels.

4. Constraints: What constraints exist in your system? Are they visible or invisible? Do they guide behavior, or do they frustrate it?

The constraints in my system are in two categories. There are the manipulation tool constraints which are comprised of the diameter of the distortion tool and the specific location of the distortion which is bound by the position of the mouse. These two constraints are relatively visible since they tangibly define the visual which is presented to the user. Outside of this, there are the constraints of the text which is that the text has to be a certain size and font and can only be something which can be typed out. These constraints do serve to guide the behavior of the user since it allows there to be legibility and a specific emphasis on type distortion for the tool.

5. The Biggest Design Weakness: If Norman evaluated your project, what would he identify as its biggest design weakness? Be honest. Be specific.

I think Norman would say that the biggest weakness of my project is absolutely the lack of instructions or implied action to take. My project presents the user with an object of focus which they are to manipulate. This manipulation is not revealed explicitly in any way nor is there anything which tells the user to press the mouse which will cause the distortion.

6. What is Your Biggest Takeaway from the Text? How do you relate this text to the idea we talked about in the beginning of the semester: IxD at a macro level is about changing human behavior? Did you like the reading, if so why? And conversely if you did not like it why?

While I did enjoy this reading quite a bit, I also found it a bit laborious at times. Maybe this is just because we’ve already covered many of these topics regarding interaction, I found the discussion of signifiers to be surface-level I guess which isn’t really a critique but instead a qualm. With this being said, I found the term “affordance” useful in clarifying purposes within design as well as the distinctions made between Industrial Design, Experience Design, Interaction Design, and Human Centered Design.
